The Last Airbender World

BA SING SE

The Eternal City of the Earth Kingdom

Sheltered behind colossal walls that have stood for centuries, Ba Sing Se is the magnificent heart of the Earth Kingdom and one of the greatest cities in the world. Vast beyond imagination and rich in culture, commerce, and history, the city welcomes travelers from every nation to experience its grand avenues, vibrant markets, and timeless traditions. From the bustling Lower Ring to the serene elegance of the Upper Ring, Ba Sing Se offers an unforgettable journey through the living soul of the Earth Kingdom.

Visitors arriving through the legendary outer gates are immediately struck by the city’s astonishing scale. Tree-lined boulevards stretch across entire districts connected by canals, bridges, and the city’s famed earthbender-operated transit system. Markets overflow with silks, spices, pottery, and foods gathered from every province of the Kingdom, while tea houses and garden courtyards offer moments of calm amid the lively streets. Cultural festivals fill the city year-round with music, dance, storytelling, and performances by some of the finest musicians and performers in the Four Nations.

No destination in Ba Sing Se is more iconic than the magnificent Royal Palace, home of the Earth King and centerpiece of the prestigious Upper Ring. Nearby, visitors can explore beautifully maintained gardens, historic ministries, and grand academies devoted to philosophy, calligraphy, and the bending arts. The city’s renowned artisan districts showcase master craftsmen whose traditions stretch back generations, while the famed Jasmine Dragon tea shop remains a beloved gathering place for travelers and locals alike. Beyond the urban districts, the great agrarian zone between the city walls demonstrates the ingenuity that has allowed Ba Sing Se to thrive independently through times of war and peace alike.

Yet beyond its monumental architecture and political importance, Ba Sing Se endures because of its people. The city is a tapestry of countless communities, traditions, and stories woven together within the safety of its ancient walls. Whether watching sunset from the quiet terraces of the Upper Ring, wandering lantern-lit streets at night, or sharing tea beside the canals, visitors soon understand why generations have called Ba Sing Se the jewel of the Earth Kingdom—a city enduring, steadfast, and eternal.
